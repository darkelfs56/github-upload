Essential and Useful Parts

The things you need to complete your awesome computer

PSU

power supply unit

Converts main AC to low-voltage regulated DC power.

How to choose the best PSU-

1. Power Output
How much power you need depends on how much the components need power. Buy a higher watt power supply to be safe as there is no wrong in having more power.

2. Efficiency
How much DC power it send to the PC and how much it will lost to heat. Typically we would recommend getting the 80 Plus certificate power supply to get the most efficiency

3. Modularity of Cables
There is 3 type of modularity of PSU.
a. Non-Modular - all cable attached.
b. Semi-Modular - essential cables pre attached
c. Full-modular - no cables pre attached
Typically, with money we go with full modular so we can plug only the cables we use thus saving space and especially helps with cable management.




Cooler fan

There's two types of cooler fan in a personal computer, there is the case fan and the cpu cooler fan

Case fan

The thing we need to consider is:

1. Fan size
It varies from 25 mm to 200 mm and above. The bigger your case, the bigger the fan you can put to accomodate the flow of air. Though the bigger the fan, the slower the speed but the less noise it makes

2. Fan speed
Fan speed affects the temperature of the PC. Bigger fans can push out and pull in more air but produce more noise. Smaller fans are faster but suck in less air or push out less than their bigger counter part

3. Bearing types
There are 3 types of bearing, namely sleeve, ball and hydronynamic
a. Hydrodynamics can lubricate themsleves and operate on the lowest noise levels but they are the highest price
b. Ball bearing can work at long period but produce more noise. More suitable for server building or if the computer is not in the same room as the user.
c. Rifle type have a shorter lifespan but it fits the compromise of cost, efficiency and noise level.



CPU cooler

Two type of CPU cooler:

1. Air coolers
Made up of combination of metal heatsinks and fans. They are the simplest form of cooling in which we only use fans to cool down the heated CPU. So we can expect some fan noise if the PC is under pressure or "load".

2. All-in-One (AIO)
Also known as closed-loop coolers, much quiter than air coolers but with more higher cooling power. They use water with a coolant inside the tubes to cool down the CPU. Though sounds better than air coolers it is much more pricier than the standard air coolers.



Thermal Paste

Thermal Paste is a compound that is a heat conductive substance applied between the heatsink and the device it's meant to cool.
The heatsink will push out excess heat energy produced by the processor away from the unit and sent it to the fan or other cooling apparatus.

How to choose?
1. Know the thermal conductivity of the paste. A liquid metal thermal paste may have a high watts per meter of surface(W/mk), whule a non-metallic thermal paster have a lower W/mk.

2. Liquid metal thermal paste is much more harder to apply because of its lower density. The non-metallic counterpart have a higher viscosirt so its like an actual paste. It is much more easier to apply compare to a liquid type.

3. Thermal paste need to be applied carefully, if it leaks to the motherboard it may damage any nearby small components.



Cables

There's not much to say about this. All cables in a Personal Computer is pretty the same unless you choose to buy coloured ones to coordinate with your build. It's all aesthetics and personal touch only. Most of the cables that are used comes from either the case or from the PSU box.
Every cable that needs power like the ATX power connection for the supplying power for the motherboard and CPU, PCIe connection for supplying power for the graphic card and also other miscellaneous parts that need power like SATA for memory and case fans needed to be properly connected to the connection in the PSU. Luckily for consumers, most cables are already labelled for convinence during production.
Plus nowadays, building a computer is easy as most components can only go one way and most of it have specific number of pins that need to be connect to.
